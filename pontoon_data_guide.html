SOW Negotiation & Cost Avoidance
The most important number in this section is total managed SOW spend — it's the denominator that makes the ROI case. Even a rough order of magnitude ($100M vs. $500M vs. $1B+) is enough to build the argument. If the exact figure is sensitive, it stays internal.
#
Data point needed
Where to find it
Owner
6
Total annual value of SOW contracts managed or advised on
The aggregate dollar value of all SOW engagements Pontoon manages, advises on, or negotiates annually — across all geographies
Range
Finance / FP&A or David Orme. If exact figure is unavailable, an order-of-magnitude range is sufficient for this exercise.

Estimated rate accuracy improvement from data-driven pricing
What % improvement in pricing outcomes is realistic when negotiating with current market rate data vs. manual benchmarking? Even 0.5–1.5% is highly conservative.

David Orme's judgment based on negotiation experience. Compare: "how often do we leave value on the table because we don't have current data?"
David

Number of SOW negotiations conducted per year

#
Data point needed
Where to find it

Number of new clients or retained accounts Pathfinder is expected to enable
Conservative estimate: how many accounts could Pathfinder differentiate Pontoon in, either winning new business or preventing churn?

Estimate
David Orme + Laura. This is a qualitative input — capture it in plain language. It becomes the risk statement in the proposal.

Cost of Inaction
This section is qualitative — no numbers needed. But it is often the most powerful part of the leadership conversation. Capturing the specific risks in plain language, from the people who own those risks, turns the proposal into a risk management decision rather than a cost decision.
#
Data point needed
Where to find it
Owner
21
Competitive risk statement
What do competitors who publish market data win that Pontoon currently doesn't? What accounts has Pontoon lost — or not won — because it couldn't show data?
Estimate
Laura + account management. Ask: "Has there been a competitive situation where market intelligence capability was a factor in the outcome?"
Laura
22
Operational risk statement
What breaks in the current manual process if Pontoon adds 20% more clients or rate card volume? Is the team already at capacity?
Estimate
Laura + rate card team. "What happens to this process if our client count grows by 20%?" is the right question to ask.
Laura
23
Reputational / client risk statement

Estimate
David Orme. He owns the SOW risk and can articulate the client relationship consequence of pricing errors in plain language.
